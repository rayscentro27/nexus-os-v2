# Trading Strategy Research Framework

Gate 1 — complete specification. Gate 2 — data integrity and realistic costs. Gate 3 — minimum sample and baseline. Gate 4 — out-of-sample/walk-forward. Gate 5 — sensitivity/regime/stability. Gate 6 — risk and operational limits. Gate 7 — independent Ray Review before any practice proposal.

Suggested research thresholds are not promises: at least 100 trades when feasible, positive expectancy after costs, profit factor above 1 only with confidence interval/context, maximum drawdown within an explicitly accepted research limit, no single window/instrument dominating results, and no catastrophic parameter cliff. Thresholds vary by strategy horizon and are review inputs, not permission to trade.

Score 0–100: specification 15, data/cost realism 15, sample quality 15, out-of-sample evidence 15, stability 15, risk controls 15, reproducibility/receipt 10. Any missing stop/defined exit, martingale/revenge rule, fabricated performance, leaked future data, or live-trading request blocks continuation.

Recommendations: reject, revise specification, collect more data, continue offline testing, or draft a tightly limited practice proposal. Never recommend funded execution from Alpha.
