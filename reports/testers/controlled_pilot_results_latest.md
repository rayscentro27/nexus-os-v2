## Results by workflow

- Login succeeded for all three synthetic personas and the client/admin boundary remained enforced.
- Dashboard comprehension showed Funding Readiness, current stage, primary blocker/next action, and Continue behavior.
- Persona A exposed the normal guided credit path, approved strategy, evidence, draft, and follow-up state.
- Persona B exposed the genuine exception and specialist-review blocker with uncertainty language and no unsafe strategy.
- Persona C exposed purchased-debt documentation and contextual evidence requirements.
- Business requirements and bankability gaps were visible with rationale and next actions.
- Inline upload succeeded with a harmless synthetic document, correct category, protected metadata linkage, and in-page success state.
- Documents Vault surfaced the upload by category and linked stage/requirement without raw storage paths or persistent public URLs.
- Clyde presented page-aware guidance, known facts, unknowns, recommendation, and readiness implication.
- Funding Readiness showed contribution areas, missing/processing requirements, blockers, next actions, and review eligibility.
- Request Review showed eligibility and no-guarantee language; no external submission was executed.
- Current controlled feedback totals: 3 records (2 medium, 1 low), all backlog-only. Persisted synthetic pilot history contains 12 such observations from repeated bounded runs; none was routed to Ray Review.

## Confusion points and observations

The controlled synthetic observations were actionable rather than release-blocking: testers should have clear primary actions when a requirement is incomplete, and the exception path should retain uncertainty language. These were recorded as medium/low backlog observations and did not change product rules.

## Metrics

The pilot captured privacy-safe counts for login, task coverage, page/next-action interactions, upload attempts/success, Clyde interactions, Request Review attempts, feedback severity, and session closeout. It did not capture credentials, account numbers, document contents, raw report text, signed URLs, or tokens.
